Understanding CO2 Emissions

Carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions are one of the primary drivers of climate change. They mainly originate from the combustion of fossil fuels, including coal, oil, and natural gas, which are used for energy production, industry, and transportation. CO2 emissions significantly contribute to the greenhouse effect, leading to global warming. This visualization allows you to explore and compare the CO2 emissions of various countries, highlighting the disparities in emission levels between nations and the factors that influence these differences, such as population size, economic activity, and industrial development. By understanding these emissions, we can better assess the global effort required to reduce CO2 and mitigate climate impacts.
